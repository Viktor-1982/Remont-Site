## Refreshing the Walls: Tile Paint, Decorative Plaster & PVC Panels

![Tile painting in the bathroom](/images/remont-vannoy/remont-vannaya-steny.png "Tile painting ‚Äî a budget-friendly way to refresh walls")

You don't need to remove old tiles ‚Äî there are three easy ways to upgrade your bathroom walls:

**1. Painting the tiles**
Perfect if the tiles are intact. Use special acrylic or epoxy tile paint designed for wet areas; always prime with a high-adhesion primer.
üí° *Matte finishes hide imperfections better than glossy ones.*

üëâ **Specialty matte paints** (sometimes called "chalk-style" for their finish) are excellent ‚Äî they dry fast, give a deep matte look, and adhere well to glossy surfaces. *Crucially, ensure the product is specifically labeled for use on tiles in wet areas.*

üîÑ *Sometimes you don't even need full repainting ‚Äî refreshing or recoloring **grout lines** alone can dramatically lift the whole bathroom.*

**2. Decorative plaster**
Covers defects and adds texture. Works over tiles but best for dry zones (e.g., the wall opposite the shower), not in direct contact with water.

**3. PVC wall panels**
Fast, durable, moisture-resistant. Modern panels with hidden locks create a seamless look.

---

### Tile Painting Checklist

1.  Clean tiles thoroughly with a degreaser.
2.  Lightly sand the surface with fine-grit sandpaper (e.g., 180-220 grit) to create a "key" for the primer.
3.  Wipe away all dust and apply a high-adhesion primer for wet areas and glossy surfaces.
4.  Choose a water-resistant paint specifically for tiles (acrylic, epoxy, or a specialized "chalk-finish" tile paint).
5.  Apply 2‚Äì3 thin coats with a roller or brush, allowing full drying time between coats as per instructions.
6.  Refresh grout lines with a grout pen if needed.
7.  Seal with 1-2 coats of a clear, waterproof protective topcoat or varnish.

üí° *Let the surface cure completely for at least 48-72 hours before exposing it to steam and water.*

## Updating the Floor: PVC Tiles, Vinyl, or Paint

![PVC floor tiles in bathroom](/images/remont-vannoy/remont-vannaya-pol.png "Modern waterproof bathroom flooring")

Budget-friendly options:

- **PVC vinyl tiles** ‚Äî click-lock or self-adhesive. 100% waterproof, durable, and easy to install.
- **Sheet vinyl (linoleum)** ‚Äî choose a 100% waterproof, non-slip version with a thick wear layer.
- **Floor paint** ‚Äî a quick refresh if the base is even and in good condition (less durable long term).

üí° *Always check the floor level ‚Äî uneven spots will be visible and can affect the longevity of the new floor.*
